6.810
Engineering Interactive Technologies (fall 2021)


Class content: Provides instruction in building cutting-edge interactive technologies, explains the underlying engineering concepts, and shows how those technologies evolved over time. Students use a studio format (i.e., extended periods of time) for constructing software and hardware prototypes. Topics include interactive technologies, such as multi-touch, augmented reality, haptics, wearables, and shape-changing interfaces. In a group project, students build their own interactive hardware/software prototypes and present them in a live demo at the end of term.

Grading

  • Lab Check-Offs and Home Works (20%)
  • Problem Sets (40%)
  • Open Ended Project (40%)
  • no exams or nano quizzes

Tutorials:

    Laser Cutting
  • recommended drawing programs
  • creating a drawing for laser cutting
  • operating the laser cutter
    Conductive Inkjet Printing
  • preparing the circuit drawing
  • inkjet printing the circuit
  • 2D printer and silver cartridges
    Spraying
  • preparing the spray inks
  • safety wear and spraying
  • cleaning the airbrush
    3D Printing
  • slicing a 3D model
  • operating the 3D Printer

Administrative


Questions during the course:

  • before coming to an OH, please post on Piazza
  • OHs are run on the 6.810 slack workspace, i.e. during an OH the teaching team will be online on slack
  • once you posted on slack during the OH, we will call you via slack video messaging
  • go to a TA OH for building oriented questions
  • go to an instructor OH for logistical / grading questions
  • please do not send us emails

Late Policy:

  • labs: labs have an automatic 5 day extension, i.e. if you didn't finish lab1 during the class time (Sept. 11), you have until (Sept. 16) to complete it; labs build on each other so make sure you finish the previous lab before we do the next one to not fall behind. If the lab is still not completed after the one week extension, we apply the same point deduction as for psets (see below).
  • psets: 1-2 days late (20% off), 3-4 days late (40% off), 5-6 days late (60% off), 7 or more days late (80% off)
  • group project: no extensions, if one team member cannot make it the rest of the team needs to fill in, this applies to all milestones and the idea, midterm, and final presentation.
  • if you have to self-quarantine, are otherwise sick, or travel for a job interview, please see below for exceptions.

Self-Quarantine Policy:

  • if you have been asked to self-quarantine or if you feel you are showing any of the Covid-19 symptoms, please post privately Piazza to let us know that you cannot come in
  • rather than asking you to come in to fabricate your files, we will fabricate them for you and bring them to your dorm so you can continue working from your room once you feel better

Sick Policy:

  • ask your S3 dean to get in touch with us
  • post privately on Piazza and explain the circumstances

Leave Policy for Job Interviews:

  • if you cannot attend class because of a job interview, please post privately on Piazza and include the job interview invitation and airplane tickets or other proof of travel and we will try to accomondate you as much as possible

Collaboration Policy:

  • except for your group project, all labs and problem sets are individual efforts
  • your need to build all the hardware yourself, create all underlying manufacturing files yourself, and write all the code yourself
  • we use the same policy as 6.031 uses, you can read it in full detail here

Enrollment vs. Listener:

  • since this is a hands-on building class, we do not allow students to attend as Listeners
  • for fall 2020, we require students to be on-campus and be willing to come to the workshop for short periods of time to fabricate parts
