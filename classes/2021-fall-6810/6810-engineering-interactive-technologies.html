6.810
Engineering Interactive Technologies (fall 2021)


Class content: Provides instruction in building cutting-edge interactive technologies, explains the underlying engineering concepts, and shows how those technologies evolved over time. Students use a studio format (i.e., extended periods of time) for constructing software and hardware prototypes. Topics include interactive technologies, such as multi-touch, augmented reality, haptics, wearables, and shape-changing interfaces. In a group project, students build their own interactive hardware/software prototypes and present them in a live demo at the end of term.

Administrative


Questions during the course:

  • before coming to an OH, please post on your slack channel
  • go to a TA OH for building oriented questions
  • go to an instructor OH for logistical / grading questions
  • please do not send us emails

Late Policy:

  • labs: have an automatic 1 week extension, e.g., if you did not finish lab1 during the class time (Sept. 8), you have until (Sept. 15 end of day) to complete it. This means you have two extra lab slots to complete the work, which will give you some time to catch up. Labs in a lab series build on each other so make sure you finish the previous lab before we do the next one to not fall behind. If the lab is still not completed after the one week extension, we apply the same point deduction as for psets (see below).
  • psets: 1-2 days late (10% off), 3-4 days late (20% off), 5-6 days late (30% off), 7-8 days late (40% off), 9-10 days late (50% off), 11-12 days late (60% off), 13-14 days late (70% off), 15-16 days late (80% off), 17-18 days late (90% off)
  • group project: no extensions, if one team member cannot make it the rest of the team needs to fill in, this applies to all milestones and the idea and final presentation.
  • if you have to self-quarantine, are otherwise sick, or travel for a job interview, please see below for exceptions.

Sick Policy:

  • ask your S3 dean to get in touch with us
  • post on your slack channel or email Prof. Mueller to explain the circumstances

Leave Policy for Job Interviews:

  • if you cannot attend class because of a job interview, please post in your slack channel or email Prof. Mueller and include the job interview invitation and airplane tickets or other proof of travel and we will try to accomondate you as much as possible

Collaboration Policy:

  • except for your group project, all labs and problem sets are individual efforts
  • you need to build all the hardware yourself, create all underlying manufacturing files yourself, and write all the code yourself
  • we use the same policy as 6.031 uses, you can read it in full detail here

Enrollment vs. Listener:

  • since this is a hands-on building class, we do not allow students to attend as Listeners
  • for fall 2021, we require students to be on-campus, there is no option to take this course remotely
