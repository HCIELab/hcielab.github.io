Basic Principle


First, take the temporary tattoo paper with its paper backing our of its packaging.
Then inkjet print the art layer and the conductive layer on top (if you print the art layer over the inkjet layer, you can hide the conductive traces).
After print-ing each layer, the ink is heat cured with a heat gun (130◦C,3 minutes).
Then add the electronic components (Mount electronics onto the circuit using z-axis conductive tape at appropriate locations if needed. Apply copper tape or any desired connector to power the circuit.).
Then apply the adhesion layer.

Materials


Tattoo Paper: inkjet-printable temporary tattoo paper (Silhouette Inkjet Printable tattoo paper, $7.42 for four 8.5”x11” sheets). (Tattoo Decal Paper)
Art Inks: simple CMY color inks.
Conductive Ink: which one is skin friendly? we decided against fabricating the Skintillates devices with inkjet printing because of the lack of data in the safety and long-term biocompatibility of these inks..
Electronic components: anything that doesn't work.
Adhesion Material: does this come with the Tattoo paper?

Cost: A Skintillates tattoo that measures 6.5 in x 1.0 in would cost $0.23 in temporary tattoo paper and adhesive. It would take approximately 0.3g of silver screen-printing ink to fabricate the circuit, which would cost $1.2. With $0.50 allocated for two surface mount LEDs, the total cost of such a device is $1.83.

Printing your Design


You need to print on the glossy side of the white sheet.
Use the printers photo paper settings if available.

Transferring the Tattoo


When ink is dry, apply the adhesive sheet (green sheet).
To apply the adhesive sheet, peel away the green backing and then place the sticky transparent film on to the top of the printed image.
Use a scraper tool or ruler to make sure the adhesive sheet is attached over the entire area, this is important so make sure you press on it properly across the entire area, otherwise parts of your tattoo will not have adhesive on them and will not stick to your skin.
You can cut out the design either using a scissor, laser cutter, or paper plotter (the Silhouette has a 'Temporary Tattoo Paper' setting).
Remove the clear plastic from the adhesive layer (yes all that's left will be the glue), which exposes the adhesive.
Apply the tattoo adhesive side to your skin and cover with a wet cloth for 10-15 seconds or until paper backing slides off easily.
