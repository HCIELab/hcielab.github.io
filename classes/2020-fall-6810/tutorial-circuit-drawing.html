Conductive Inkjet Printing: Preparing the Circuit Drawing


Set Document Color Mode to CMYK: Since we will print our document later, please set the document color mode to CMYK (not RGB, which is for on-screen documents).

Set Units to mm: First, if your drawing is still set to 'pts' as a measurement unit, set it to mm or inch.

Color of Lines: The color for all your circuit design should be set to black (CMYK: 0,0,0,255) since the 'K' cartridge will contain our silver ink.

Create a New Layer: Make sure all your circuit design is on a new layer, i.e. later we need to export two files seperately, the visual design in one pdf and the circuit design in a seperate pdf. Using a seperate layer for each allows you to turn each part of the design on/off quickly for export.

Area of Touch Button: A touch button should be large enough for a user's finger (size of a finger tip is an area around 2cm x 2cm).

Thickness of Wires: The thicker the wire, the more conductive. Wire thickness between 0.35mm and 1mm are recommended. We typically draw 'rectangles' for the wires rather than 'lines' since a rectangle allows us to see its 'width' in the shape properties. The thickness of the wire determines its conductivity (larger cross-section = hire conductivity), with 1mm we are on the safe side given the conductive ink we use and its resistance.

Connector Pads for Wires: One end of your wire connects to your touch button, the other end of your wire will connect to your connector pad, which we will then connect to the microcontroller. The connector pad for the microcontroller needs to be large enough to connect to conductive tape, i.e. it should be 1cm x 1cm to be on the safe side. Make sure your connector pads are as close to the boundary of the card as possible. We will later connect them to copper tape for connecting to the circuit, if they are too far away the crocodile clamps cannot reach them (left image below is not good, right side is better).
