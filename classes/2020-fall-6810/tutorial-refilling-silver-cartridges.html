Conductive Inkjet Printing: 2D Printer and Silver Cartridges

Buying Materials and Preparing the Printer


While we have already done this for you, we provide you here with the details in case you want to try this yourself in the future:

2D Printer: For the class, we are using the Canon PIXMA TR150 Printer for the silver inkjet printing, and the Epson EcoTank ET-2760 All-in-One Supertank Printer for normal color printing. We tested both printers and they will all work for the silver inkjet printing (other 2D printers may work as well but it depends on their nozzle diameter and print head type, i.e. the silver may clock them if they are too small and the print head should be Piezoelectric Print Heads.

Color Inks: Regular CMYK cartridges from here (add link).

Silver Ink: We bought empty cartridges from here (add link) and seperately bought silver ink from Mitsubishi.

Syringe: To fill the silver into the cartridge, you need a syringe. We bought syringens from here (add link)

Paper: With the silver ink we use, we cannot print on any paper but need a special sheet, which chemically sinters the silver ink once it adheres to the sheet. We use the following sheets: Mitsubishi PET films and Kodak glossy photo paper.

Drying out: Note that if the silver is too long in the cartridge, it will eventually dry out and not print anymore.
