6.810 (former 6.S063)
Engineering Interactive Technologies (fall 2018)


Everything on this page is provisional until announced in class.

Class content: Provides instruction in building cutting-edge interactive technologies, explains the underlying engineering concepts, and shows how those technologies evolved over time. Students use a studio format (i.e., extended periods of time) for constructing software and hardware prototypes. Topics include interactive technologies, such as multi-touch, augmented reality, haptics, wearables, and shape-changing interfaces. In a group project, students build their own interactive hardware/software prototypes and present them in a live demo at the end of term. Enrollment may be limited.

Grading:
  • open ended project (1-2 students), including midterm and final presentation (60%)
  • problem sets (40%)
  • no nanoquizzes and no exams
