Goal

We are going to show you how you can find the latest HCI research projects yourself so when class is over you still know how to stay up to date.

HCI Conferences

The top conference in HCI and ACM CHI (ca. 3000 attendees, everything from usability studies to augmented reality devices) and ACM UIST (technical HCI research, most of the stuff you see in class is from the UIST conference).



Using the Conference Website

A good way to get started is to checkout the conference website.
Here's this at the example of UIST 2016:



For each session you can see the paper titles + authors.



If you click on a paper, you can even get a 30 second video preview.



Try this here on the UIST 2018 website (yes, this is where the teaching team is right now :))!.

http://confer.csail.mit.edu/uist2018/schedule
(scroll to one of the paper sessions, e.g. monday, 11.30 - 12.50, click on a paper title to see the video).

Getting the Full paper PDF

So now you know how to see what is presented this year.
But how do you get the full paper pdf (10 pages)?
For this, you have to go to the ACM Digital Library (ACM = Association for Computing Machinary) that stores all conference proceedings.
