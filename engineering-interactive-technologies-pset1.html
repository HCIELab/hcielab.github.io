6.S063 Engineering Interactive Technologies (fall 2017)

PSet1: Build a Multi-Touch Pad

Deadline: due at 1pm on Wednesday, September 27, 2017

Deliverables:

  • upload a short video of your touchpad + the application in action to Gradebook
  • upload finger tracking + application code to Gradebook
  • give a live demo of your touch pad to your TA in class

Task:
Build a multi-touch pad using the principles of FTIR (Frustrated Total Internal Reflection).
The multi-touch pad has all the components required later for your group project: custom laser-cut parts, custom electronics, and custom code.
We hope by doing this problem set and getting experience with end-to-end prototyping, you will be able to better scope your group project.


Provided Resources:
We will provide you with all the materials you need, you will get from us:

  • an acrylic sheet
  • a webcam
  • LEDs
  • a switch
  • resistors
  • USB connector + cover
  • cables

In addition we give you the following components, which help you prototype your touchpad but are not part of the final prototype:

  • an Arduino Nano
  • a breadboard
  • jumper wires

Equipment:
To build the multi-touch pad you need access to the following equipment:

  • a laser cutter
  • a heat gun or strip heater for bending the sheet
  • a soldering iron

Instructions for Building the Hardware:

  • create the .svg file for the acrylic sheet and laser cut it
    • multi-touch pad height: XXcm, tracking area on top: YYcm x ZZcm (see drawing)
    • with 8 LED holes
    • with 1 hole for the switch
    • optional: add thin engraved lines for easier bending
  • use strip heater or heat gun to bend the acrylic sheet
  • prototype LED setup using the breadboard and the Arduino
    • test 1 LED first with the corresponding resistor first, lights up? good.
    • extend your circuit for 8 LEDs, adjust resistor, all light up? good
  • remove LEDs from breadboard and plug them into your acrylic sheet
  • cross LED legs to connect them to each other
  • test with Arduino 5V and GND if they still all light up
  • if yes, solder them
  • add switch to the setup, solder the switch to the LEDs
  • test with Arduino 5V and GND, does the switch work?
  • assembly your USB cable
  • create your USB cable
  • connect everything to your laptop, does the LEDs light up (+ switch is on ;))?
  • tape the camera on, you area ready for the computer vision part

Instructions for Writing the Software:

  • display camera image
  • convert to HSV color space
  • threshold for your LED color
    • it helps to create some sliders for hmin, hmax, smin, smax, vmin, vmax so you can try around until you find good values
  • find contours
  • delete contours that are smaller than a finger blob
  • find contour center to have touch coordinates
    • fit ellipse around contour
    • get center of ellipse
  • draw (x,y) coordinates of each finger tip onto the image
  • now that you have the finger positions, add some of your own code, e.g. a gesture recognition (zoom in with two fingers) or a drawing app, feel free to get createive here. extra points for clever applications.
